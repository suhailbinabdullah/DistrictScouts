AIMS AND OBJECTIVES OF DISTRICT ASSOCIATION

It is said that the best way to teach youth to be good is to guide and prepare them in doing good. Practice and preaching should go together or better still practice should precede precept. This is the aim & objective of this creative Movement called Scouting & Guiding. It, therefore, aims at to provide young boys and girls (Bunnies, Cubs/Bulbuls, Scouts/Guides, Rovers/Rangers and Scouters/Guiders) opportunities to ;

  • have a useful and enjoyable time in the open air, outside school hours ;

  • have training and guidance in the habits of self-control, self-help, self-reliance and self-sacrifice ;

  • find an environment for character building, so that they become better men/women and citizens, not only of their mother land but of the whole-world ;

  • develop in them the spirit of seva-service through altruistic living i.e. living for others and also the civic sense in order to live purer, nobler and healthier lives ;

  • cultivate in them honour, commitment, dedication, conviction, courtesy, loyalty, obedience, trust worthiness, integrity and grace ;

  • show reverence for the great ones, the seers and sages, the heroes and the Holy men of their own religion, of other faiths, other religions, & other countries and above the humanity ;

  • form sound health habits ;

  • provide training in some handicrafts and skills of daily use ;

  • inculcate in them a habit of doing good turn. The Scout method is eminently practical “DO” rather than “Read about” is its characteristics

  • dilate on the spirit of goodwill, mutual understanding, tolerance, truth and justice in place of envy, hatred and malice ;

  • have in them a sense of responsibility and trust worthiness and to develop initiative and leadership qualities in them ; and

  • imbibe spiritual qualities by opening their spiritual nature as this movement believes that man is essentially a spirit. He is not a body. He is not body, mind complex, he is a social human being.
