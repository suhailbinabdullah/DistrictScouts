Scouting in Jammu and Kashmir

Scouting was inexistence before 1931 ie. in thirties in J&K State. His Highness Maharaja Shri Hari SIngh Ji Bhadur was the Chief Scout where as Khan Bhadur Raja Mohammed Afzal Khan, the then Home Minister, was the State Scout Commissioner, the testimony of this fact is borne by i) The civil list published on Ist Kartik, 1998. ii) Shri Abdul Rashid Khan Brother of Shri N.A. Khan (State Chief Commissioner J&K Bharat Scouts & Guides), who was born in 1923 and was active till his last breath. He was a Cub Scout in Govt. M.S. Baramula in 1932. He passed Tenderfoot test in May, 1935 as a Scout of Govt. H.S. Srinagar situated at Bagh Dilawar Khan and continued as a Scout upto 1938. He did his M.Sc. (Zoology) and joined Forest Department and retired as a Chief Conservater of Forests J&K State. He is a living legend full of Scout Spirit and is considered as a great celebrity and luminary of the State.

After the Independence the association became defunct in the wake of Uphavels that followed it. The state Department of Education, at the Government level, revived the scout Movement in the State in the year- 1959. While appreciating the good work being done by Youth Welfare Department. the then National Commissioner, Bharat Scouts and Guides wrote to the Chief Minister and the Chief Secretary J&K State that the movement needed to have a "Voluntary Character" on the pattern of other states of the country so that eminent people from different walks of life and Youth from Colleges, Public , Pvt Schools are associated with the promotion of the movement and also the non-student youths are involved. With this objective in view, the State Government Vide G.O. No 755-Education of 1987 dated 31-03-1987 allowed the movement to function as a voluntary association. In the beginning adhoc association was formed and since 1993 regular elections are held under Bye Laws and Constitution His Excellency, the Governor of J&K State is its patron and Hon'ble Chief Minister is its vice-patron. 

The association fervently hopes that it will be given credit that it has been able to accomplish the well envisaged targets inspite of many hurdles and barriers since 1989 to this date. 

It has been trying to cover more and more neglected areas of the state in all the districts. The present Census is very encouraging as it has gone over 50000 from Bunnies to Rovers & Rangers with the effective, meaningful, sympathetic and rational support of the Education and Youth Services and Sports Department. Almost, in all the districts of the State, Bharat Scouts and Guides Association has its units and it is really a very encouraging feature, inspite of the facts, that has not been able to have due financial, moral, physical a motivational support from the Government, the Society the families though-Scouting is a most important Non-formal education which generates education in values in a more useful and lasting manner as no education can be complete without value oriented programmes and activities.

The present State Chief Commissioner is Shri. Nazir Ahmed Khan (Rtd Director General, YS&S, J&K)
